Expansion Joints

Expansion joints are used in a variety of structures, including buildings, bridges, and roads, to accommodate thermal expansion and contraction caused by changes in temperature. They allow the structure to move without causing damage or stress to the materials. They are typically installed between sections of the structure, such as between concrete slabs or between sections of a bridge, to allow for expansion and contraction without causing cracking or other damage. They can also be used to isolate sections of a structure from vibrations caused by trains or other heavy machinery.

Elastomeric Bearing

Elastomeric bearings are used in the construction of bridges and other structures as a means of supporting and isolating the superstructure from the substructure. They work by providing a flexible support that can accommodate movement and deformation of the structure, while also reducing stress on the substructure and protecting it from damage. They are also used in seismic isolation systems to reduce the transmission of seismic energy to the structure above

Conveyor Belts

Conveyor belts are used to transport materials or products from one place to another. They consist of a continuous loop of flexible material, such as rubber or plastic, that rotates around two or more pulleys. Conveyor belts can be powered or gravity-driven. They are used in a variety of settings such as manufacturing and assembly plants, distribution centers, and mines. They are used to move products through a production line, transport goods in a warehouse, or take ore out of a mine. They are also used in airports and other transportation hubs to move baggage and other items. They are known for their reliability, durability and versatility and can be designed to handle a variety of products and materials.

Interlock Mould

Interlock mould, also known as interlocking paving stone mould, is a type of mould used to create interlocking concrete pavers. These pavers are commonly used in outdoor areas such as driveways, patios, sidewalks, and garden paths. They are designed to be easy to install and provide a durable, low-maintenance surface that can withstand heavy foot and vehicle traffic.
